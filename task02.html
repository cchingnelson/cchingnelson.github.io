Islamabad (/ɪzˈlɑːməbæd/ (listen);[9] Urdu: اسلام آباد, romanized: Islām ābād) is the capital city of Pakistan, and is administered by the Pakistani federal government as part of the Islamabad Capital Territory.
. It is the ninth-largest city in Pakistan, while the larger Islamabad–Rawalpindi metropolitan area is the country's third-largest with a population of about 4.1 million people.
Built as a planned city in the 1960s, it replaced Rawalpindi as Pakistan's capital. Islamabad is noted for its high standards of living,[10] safety,[1] and abundant greenery.[11]
